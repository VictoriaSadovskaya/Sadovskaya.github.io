{\Large {\bf Similarity dimension.}
\vskip.4cm

{\large
A line segment, a square, and a cube have dimensions 1, 2, and 3, respectively.
\vskip.2cm
How many copies of them are needed to build a copy scaled by a factor of 2\,?
\vskip.1cm
\hskip1cm Interval: $2^1=2$, \, \, square: $2^2=4$, \, \, cube: $2^3=8$
\vskip.2cm
How many copies of them are needed to build a copy scaled by a factor of 3\,?
\vskip.1cm
\hskip1cm  Interval: $ 3^1=3$, \, \, square: $3^2=9$, \, \, cube: $3^3=27$
 \vskip.5cm



If  $N$ copies of an object are needed to build its copy scaled by a factor of $S$
\vskip.1cm
{\it (if an object is built of $N$ copies of itself scaled by a factor of $\,1/S$)},
\vskip.1cm
 then its \,{\bf similarity dimension}\,  is the number $d\,$  such that \,
\vskip.1cm
$S^d=N$,\,  that is, $\,d=\frac{\ln N}{\ln S}$.
\vskip.3cm

\vskip.3cm (!) This definition applies only to a narrow class of sets.


\vskip.8cm


Cantor set:   $\hskip2.9cm N=2,\quad S=3,\quad 3^d=2, \quad d=\frac {\ln 2}{\ln 3} \approx 0.63$
\vskip.5cm

Koch curve:   $\hskip2.7cm N= 4,\quad S= 3, \quad 3^d=4, \quad d=\frac {\ln 4}{\ln 3} \approx 1.26$
\vskip.5cm

1 $<d\,$(Sierpinski triangle) $< d\,$(Sierpinski carpet) $<2$
\vskip.3cm 
$2 < d\,$(Menger sponge) $<3$  \hskip3.5cm {\it Compute these dimensions.}
}
